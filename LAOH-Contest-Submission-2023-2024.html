Constance Markievicz: A Heroine in Ireland's Struggle for Independence

Constance Markievicz was born February 4, 1868, in London, England and she died July 15, 1927, Dublin, Ireland

Markievicz's involvement in the Easter Rising of 1916 marked a turning point in her life. She fought alongside her fellow Irish republicans, enduring hardships and dangers in pursuit of a free Ireland. Her dedication to the cause extended beyond the battlefield; she was a co-founder of Cumann na mBan, a women's republican organization.

Markievicz's legacy as a feminist and revolutionary remains influential, and her story serves as a reminder of the strength of character and determination that can shape history.
